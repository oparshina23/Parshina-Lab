What We Study

Research Areas

Our lab uses eye-tracking, scanpath analysis, and behavioral experiments to study how people read and process language ‚Äî across languages, populations, and levels of proficiency.

Eye Movements & Reading

We use eye-tracking to capture the real-time dynamics of reading across languages and scripts. By recording fixation durations, regressions, and scanpaths, we examine how visual and linguistic information interact during sentence processing. This approach allows us to identify subtle differences in processing efficiency, lexical access, and integration across reader populations and language contexts.

üåç

Bilingualism

How do bilinguals manage two languages while reading? We study language control, predictive processing, and even how bilinguals process jokes ‚Äî with implications for both theory and language pedagogy.

üìñ

Reading Development & Dyslexia

What distinguishes typical from atypical reading development? We examine global reading patterns and scanpaths in children at risk for dyslexia, and explore phonological deficits and their neural correlates.

üîÆ

Heriage Languages

We study a special group of bilingual speakers, heritage speakers, individuals who grow up exposed to a minority language at home while living in a majority language environment. These speakers typically acquire two languages from early childhood, yet their linguistic experience differs in systematic ways from both monolingual speakers and late second language learners.
